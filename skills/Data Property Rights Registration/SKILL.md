# Skill Name: Data Property Rights Registration
## Core Problem
Register data assets with a formal authority to establish legal proof of ownership and protect data property rights.
## S_route
| Intent | Action | Resource |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Identify Data Assets | Inventory and catalog all data assets (structured, semi-structured, unstructured) with metadata (name, source, format, volume, update frequency, retention period). | Data inventory tools, asset management system |
| Classify Data Type | Categorize each asset as personal, business, public, or derived data to determine the applicable legal framework. | Data classification guidelines, legal framework reference (GDPR, CCPA, PIPL) |
| Determine Ownership Rights | Examine contracts, laws, and regulations to establish the legal ownership chain and resolve ambiguities. | Contractual agreements, legal review team, data protection laws |

| Issue Registration Certificate | Receive and verify the official certificate (unique ID, owner details, classification, validity, restrictions) via the authority’s online registry. | Authority’s online registry portal |
## S_body
1. Identify Data Assets – Inventory and catalog all data assets held by the organization or individual, including structured data (databases, spreadsheets), semi-structured data (JSON, XML, logs), and unstructured data (documents, images, audio). For each asset, record: name, source, format, volume, update frequency, and retention period.
2. Classify Data Type – Categorize each data asset according to its nature: personal data (PII, sensitive personal information), business data (trade secrets, financial records, intellectual property), public data (open government data, publicly available datasets), or derived data (aggregated, anonymized, or processed data). This classification determines which legal framework applies.
3. Determine Ownership Rights – Establish the legal ownership chain by examining: contractual agreements (employment contracts, service terms, data licensing agreements), applicable data protection laws (GDPR, CCPA, PIPL, etc.), industry regulations, and any joint ownership or co-ownership arrangements. Resolve ambiguities through legal review.
4. Register with Authority – Submit a registration application to the designated data property rights authority or registry body. The application typically includes: data asset description, classification certificate, ownership proof documents, purpose of registration (commercial use, licensing, protection), and any associated metadata standards. Pay applicable registration fees and comply with jurisdictional requirements.
5. Issue Registration Certificate – Upon successful review and approval, the authority issues an official Data Property Rights Registration Certificate containing: unique registration number, data asset identifier, registered owner details, classification level, registration date and validity period, and any usage restrictions or conditions. The certificate serves as legal proof of data property rights.

## S_params
- data_asset_metadata: Name, source, format, volume, update frequency, and retention period for each data asset.
- classification_level: Personal, business, public, or derived data type.
- ownership_proof: Contractual agreements, legal documents, and ownership chain evidence.
- registration_purpose: Commercial use, licensing, or protection.
- jurisdiction: Applicable legal framework and geographic region for registration.
- registration_fee: Applicable fees required by the authority.
- certificate_id: Unique registration number for verification.
- validity_period: Duration for which the registration certificate is valid.
